# Monsta Pipeline — Issue Tracker

This repository is the central place to report bugs and request features for Monsta Studios' internal pipeline tools. It covers:

- **Monsta Tool** — the in-house pipeline suite
- **Monsta Submission** — the render submission tool
- **Kitsu** — production tracking
- Any other pipeline-adjacent tooling used in the studio

All departments are welcome to report — Layout, Asset, Animation, Rendering, Lighting, FX, and Compositing. Outsource staff can also submit reports.

---

## Before You Submit

Search the [existing issues](https://github.com/MonstaStudios/monsta_pipeline_issues/issues) first. If your problem is already reported, leave a comment with any additional detail rather than opening a duplicate.

---

## How to Report

### If you have a GitHub account

Use the native issue form and choose the appropriate template:

- **[Bug Report](https://github.com/MonstaStudios/monsta_pipeline_issues/issues/new?template=bug_report.md)** — something is broken or behaving unexpectedly
- **[Feature Request](https://github.com/MonstaStudios/monsta_pipeline_issues/issues/new?template=feature_request.md)** — you want a new feature or improvement

> To open links in a new tab, right-click (or Ctrl+click on Mac) and choose "Open link in new tab."

### If you do not have a GitHub account

Use the external form: **[issue-form.monsta.my](https://issues-form.monsta.my/)**

Your submission will be posted automatically by the issue bot. Include your contact info if you want a follow-up.

---

## What to Include

Regardless of which method you use, a good report should always have:

| Field | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Your name and department** | Helps us follow up and understand context |
| **PC No.** (e.g. MON 042) | Lets us check machine-specific configs if needed |
| **Which tool is affected** | Narrows down where to look |
| **Steps to reproduce** | Without these, we cannot reliably confirm or fix the bug |
| **Screenshots or logs** | Speeds up diagnosis significantly |

The more specific you are, the faster the issue gets resolved.

---

## What Happens After You Submit

1. The pipeline team reviews and labels the issue (e.g. `bug`, `enhancement`, `needs-info`).
2. If more information is needed, a team member will comment on the issue.
3. Issues are prioritized and assigned to the relevant milestone.
4. Once resolved, the issue is closed with a note on what changed.

If you submitted via the external form and included contact info, you may be reached directly.

---

## Labels

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `bug` | Confirmed broken behavior |
| `enhancement` | Requested improvement or new feature |
| `needs-info` | Waiting on more detail from the reporter |
| `in-progress` | Actively being worked on |
| `wontfix` | Out of scope or intentional behavior |

---

## Contact

For urgent production-blocking issues, reach the pipeline team directly through your department supervisor or internal chat rather than waiting on GitHub.
